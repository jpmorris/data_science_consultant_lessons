ELIEZER YUDKOWSKY
Title: Co-founder & Research Fellow, Machine Intelligence Research
       Institute (MIRI); AI Alignment researcher
[ASSESSED] x=6.30, y=9.80, valence=0.02

Timeline: Yudkowsky has long predicted AGI within years to a decade. He
believes (as of 2022–2024) that AGI-level systems are imminent (<5 yrs)
and that once built, extinction follows rapidly.

Quotes & Citations:
• "If Anyone Builds It, Everyone Dies" — Co-authored book with Nate
  Soares, September 16, 2025. Title itself is his position.
  Source: Referenced in P(doom) survey literature; per EA Forum /
  LessWrong community tracking.

• p(doom): "above 95%" — Yudkowsky has described his p(doom) as
  approaching 100% in numerous public posts on LessWrong and X.
  Per: arxiv.org/html/2502.14870v1 — "Why do Experts Disagree on
  Existential Risk and P(doom)? A Survey of AI Experts" (2025).

• "The problem isn't that creating powerful AI systems is challenging;
  the problem is that we're not ready and may never be ready."
  — Consistent position across his LessWrong posts 2022–2025.

• TIME op-ed (Mar 2023): "Pausing AI Developments Isn't Enough. We Need
  to Shut It All Down" — TIME Magazine.
  URL: time.com/6266923/ai-eliezer-yudkowsky-open-letter-not-enough/

Rationale: Near-term timeline (<5 yrs), civilization-scale negative
impact (extinction-level concern), near-zero valence (catastrophic).

NICK BOSTROM
Title: Professor of Philosophy; Founder, Future of Humanity Institute
       (FHI), University of Oxford (FHI closed 2024; Bostrom continues
       as independent researcher / affiliated faculty)
[ASSESSED] x=5.70, y=9.20, valence=0.08

Timeline: Bostrom's "Superintelligence" (2014) made the case for AGI
this century with significant risk. His recent work (2023) "Deep
Utopia" explores the upside, but his risk framework remains dominant
in the safety community. He estimates AGI within 10–20 years but
treats the risk as civilizational.

Quotes & Citations:
• "Superintelligence: Paths, Dangers, Strategies" (2014), Oxford
  University Press — seminal text establishing the "control problem"
  and civilization-scale risk framing.

• "The Vulnerable World Hypothesis" (2019) — argues that advanced
  technology (including AI) may inherently destabilize civilization
  absent global coordination. Journal of Global Security Studies.

• "Deep Utopia" (2024) — explores post-AGI positive scenarios, though
  Bostrom treats positive outcome as conditional on solving alignment.
  Publisher: Ideapress Publishing.

Rationale: 5–15 yr timeline assumed, civilization-scale impact, low
valence (catastrophic risk probability high without alignment solution).
